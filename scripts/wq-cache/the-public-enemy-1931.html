The Public Enemy is a 1931 film about two mobsters, Tom Powers and his lifelong friend Matt Doyle and their rise to power in prohibition-era America.

Directed by William A. Wellman. Written by Harvey F. Thew, based on Beer and Blood by John Bright and Kubec Glasmon.
