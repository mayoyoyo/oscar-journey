Throne of Blood is a 1957 Japanese jidaigeki film about a war-hardened general, egged on by his ambitious wife, who works to fulfill a prophecy that he would become lord of Spider's Web Castle. The film transposes the plot of William Shakespeare's play Macbeth (1606) from medieval Scotland to feudal Japan, with stylistic elements drawn from Noh drama.

Directed by Akira Kurosawa. Written by Shinobu Hashimoto, Ryūzō Kikushima, Akira Kurosawa, and Hideo Oguni, based on Macbeth by William Shakespeare.

Lady Asaji Washizu: There is still blood. My hands. Why do they not become clean? I wash and wash, still they smell of blood.
